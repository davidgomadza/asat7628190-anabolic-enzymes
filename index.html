📚 Overview of Anabolic Enzymes

Anabolic enzymes catalyze biochemical reactions that synthesize larger complex molecules from smaller units [[2]]. These enzymes harness energy from ATP or other high-energy molecules to drive synthetic reactions forward [[4]].

🏗️ Anabolic Pathways

Requires Energy (ATP)

Build up large molecules from smaller ones through biosynthesis. Examples include protein synthesis, steroid biosynthesis, and DNA replication [[8]].

🔨 Catabolic Pathways

Produces Energy (ATP)

Break down complex molecules into simpler ones, releasing energy. Examples include glycolysis and beta-oxidation.

🧬 The Mevalonate Pathway

Steroid biosynthesis follows the mevalonate pathway that uses acetyl-CoA as building blocks to form steroids from simple precursors [[11]].

1

Starting Materials

Acetyl-CoA
C₂₃H₃₈N₇O₁₇P₃S
Acetyl-CoA Acetyltransferase

Two molecules of acetyl-CoA condense to form acetoacetyl-CoA, the first step in the mevalonate pathway.

2

HMG-CoA Formation

HMG-CoA
HMG-CoA Synthase

Acetoacetyl-CoA combines with another acetyl-CoA to form 3-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl-CoA (HMG-CoA).

3

Mevalonate Production

Mevalonate
HMG-CoA Reductase

Rate-limiting step: HMG-CoA is reduced to mevalonate. This is the committed step in cholesterol biosynthesis.

4

Isoprenoid Units

IPP & DMAPP
Mevalonate Kinase Phosphomevalonate Kinase

Mevalonate is phosphorylated and decarboxylated to form isopentenyl pyrophosphate (IPP) and dimethylallyl pyrophosphate (DMAPP).

5

Squalene Formation

Squalene
Squalene Synthase

IPP and DMAPP are coupled to form geranyl pyrophosphate (GPP), then farnesyl pyrophosphate (FPP), and finally squalene (C₃₀).

6

Lanosterol Cyclization

Lanosterol
Squalene Epoxidase Oxidosqualene Cyclase

Squalene is epoxidized to 2,3-oxidosqualene, which undergoes electrophilic cyclization to form lanosterol, the first steroid in the pathway [[11]].

7

Cholesterol Synthesis

Cholesterol
Multiple Enzymes

Lanosterol undergoes multiple conversions including removal of three methyl groups and double bond rearrangements to form cholesterol (C₂₇).

⚙️ Key Enzymes in Anabolic Pathways

CYP11A1 (P450scc)

Cholesterol side-chain cleavage enzyme that converts cholesterol (C₂₇) to pregnenolone (C₂₁), the first and rate-limiting step in steroid hormone biosynthesis [[14]].

CYP17A1

Bifunctional enzyme with 17α-hydroxylase and 17,20-lyase activities. Responsible for converting C₂₁ steroids to C₁₉ androgens [[14]].

Aromatase (CYP19A1)

Catalyzes the oxidative removal of the C19 methyl group and aromatization of ring A to convert androgens to estrogens (C₁₈ steroids) [[14]].

Squalene Epoxidase

Catalyzes the regioselective epoxidation of squalene to 2,3-oxidosqualene using FADH₂ and molecular oxygen. Key step before cyclization [[11]].

HMG-CoA Reductase

The rate-limiting enzyme in the mevalonate pathway. Converts HMG-CoA to mevalonate. Target of statin drugs.

5α-Reductase

Converts testosterone to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a more potent androgen, through reduction of the Δ4 double bond.

📝 Test Your Knowledge

📊 Key Facts About Anabolic Enzyme Production

  • All enzymes are proteins, and their synthesis involves linking amino acids in the correct sequence [[3]]
  • Enzyme synthesis can be induced by the presence of substrates or hormones like steroids and thyroid hormones [[5]]
  • The biosynthesis of steroids follows a unique pathway in animals compared to many other organisms [[11]]
  • Squalene epoxidation is catalyzed by squalene epoxidase using flavin hydroperoxide as the oxidizing agent [[11]]
  • The polyene cyclization involves stereospecific anti-periplanar addition across three C=C bonds [[11]]
  • Steroid hormones include C₂₁ (progesterone, cortisol), C₁₉ (androgens), and C₁₈ (estrogens) compounds [[14]]
